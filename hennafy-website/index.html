FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before your first appointment.

Henna is never black — it always stains a rich burgundy-brown tone. If someone offers you "black henna," that's a chemical dye that can cause serious skin reactions. We only use 100% natural, organic henna. For a darker look, we offer Jagua, a tropical fruit-based alternative that produces a beautiful dark blue-black color.
Jagua (Genipa americana) is a tropical fruit from the Amazon rainforest. It produces a dark blue-black stain that lasts 2–3 weeks — similar duration to henna. The application time is shorter (about 2–3 hours vs. 8–10 hours for henna). Note: if you're allergic to berries, please avoid Jagua and consult us first.
We use the highest-grade organic henna powder sourced from India and Pakistan, mixed with water, sugar, and essential oils — sweet orange, lavender, and Cajeput. Sometimes eucalyptus is used; please let us know if you have any essential oil sensitivities. All ingredients are 100% natural with zero chemicals or PPD.
Henna and Jagua typically last 2–3 weeks. To maximize longevity: leave the paste on for 8–10 hours, spray with a lemon-sugar solution every hour while it's on, and avoid water for at least 8 hours after removal. Avoid chlorine, excessive hand washing, and alcohol-based products. The stain reaches its darkest 36–48 hours after removal.
Arrive with clean, lotion-free skin — no oils, creams, or makeup on the areas being hennaed. If you're getting both hands, feet, or a back design, bring a driver so we don't waste time waiting for you to dry. Wear comfortable clothing that allows easy access to the area.
